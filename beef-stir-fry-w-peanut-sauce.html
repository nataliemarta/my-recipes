Beef Stir-Fry w/ Peanut Sauce

Serves: 4  ·  Total Time: 35 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons peanut butter
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 3 teaspoons olive or sesame oil
  • 1 pound skirt or flank steak, cut into thin slices
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced or pressed
  • 3-4 cups chopped vegetables, such as broccoli, carrots, snap peas, and peppers
  • Green tops of chopped green onions, optional for garnish
  • Chopped peanuts, optional for garnish
  • Serve with: White or brown rice

Directions

  1. Place soy sauce, rice vinegar, peanut butter, and brown sugar in a small bowl and whisk until smooth. Set aside.
  2. Heat 2 teaspoons of sesame or olive oil in a large skillet. Add sliced beef and cook until browned, stirring frequently. Remove from pan and drain any liquid from pan.
  3. Return pan to stove and heat remaining teaspoon of sesame oil. Add garlic and cook, stirring constantly, about 15 seconds. Add vegetables, stirring and tossing frequently until crisp-tender and still brightly colored (about four minutes).
  4. Add beef back to pan with veggies and stir. Add sauce to pan and stir until everything is combined.
  5. Serve on top of rice and garnish each serving with chopped green onions and chopped peanuts, if desired.
Picky Eater TipPlate the beef, rice, and veggies separately, allowing your kids to build their plate the way they prefer. Keep some veggies uncooked if they prefer raw.
