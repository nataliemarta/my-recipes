Easy Quiche

Serves: 6  ·  Total Time: 50 minutes

Ingredients

  • 4 eggs
  • 1/2 cup half-and-half
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • pinch of black pepper
  • 1/4 lb cooked breakfast sausage
  • 1 cup frozen loose leaf spinach (or 1 cup chopped fresh baby spinach, loosely packed)
  • 1 9" frozen pie crust
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • Serve with: Fresh fruit, fruit smoothies, or green salads

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F.
  2. In a small bowl, beat the eggs with the half-and-half, salt, and pepper. Set aside.
  3. In a skillet over medium heat, brown sausage until no longer pink. Add frozen or fresh spinach and cook together until spinach is warmed and wilted.
  4. Put the frozen crust (in the pan) on a baking sheet. Spoon sausage mixture into pie crust, and sprinkle all over with the cheese.
  5. Whisk the egg mixture one more time, then slowly pour it into the crust, evenly and gently filling the space between the ingredients, without lifting them out of place.
  6. Place the quiche in the oven to bake until eggs are fully set, about 30 minutes. The center should be firm-looking, not watery.
  7. Cool 10 minutes before cutting. The surface of the quiche may "deflate" a bit during cooling.
  8. Note: Either freeze the uncooked portion of sausage to cook another time or brown all of it and stash the extra in the fridge or freezer for another meal.
Picky Eater TipUse familiar meats & veggies they like best. Or scramble an egg separately for them, which they can sprinkle with the cheese and serve the meat on the side.
