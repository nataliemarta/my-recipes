Turkey Lettuce Cups w/ Peanut Sauce

Serves: 4  ·  Total Time: 20 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • 2 green onions, whites and greens chopped and divided
  • 1 squirt ginger paste (or 1 tsp ginger)
  • 1 lb ground turkey (or ground chicken)
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • salt and pepper
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 teaspoons rice vinegar
  • 2 limes (1 zested & juiced, 1 cut into wedges)
  • 1/4 cup peanuts, chopped
  • 12 lettuce leaves
  • 1 carrot, cut into thin matchsticks
  • 3 radishes, sliced thin
  • Serve with: Fruit and/or store-bought Asian-style appetizers like dumplings

Directions

  1. Heat olive oil in a medium skillet over medium heat. Add the green onion whites and a generous squirt of ginger paste. Cook & stir about 1 minute.
  2. Add ground turkey, diced pepper, and a generous pinch of salt and pepper. Cook until turkey is no longer pink and cooked through and the peppers are slightly softened. Remove from heat and drain excess moisture.
  3. While the turkey is cooking, combine sauce ingredients in a small bowl (peanut butter through rice vinegar).
  4. Reserve 1/4 of the sauce for serving and add remaining sauce to the cooked turkey mixture, plus the lime juice, zest, and peanuts. Stir well to combine.
  5. Spoon turkey mixture into the lettuce cups. Drizzle each one with a small spoonful of the reserved sauce plus a squeeze of lime wedge. Top with green onions, plus thinly sliced carrots and radishes.
Picky Eater TipServe the filling on small tortillas if your kids prefer that to lettuce.
