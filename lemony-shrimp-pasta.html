Lemony Shrimp Pasta

Serves: 4  ·  Total Time: 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 12 oz. fresh or frozen uncooked, peeled shrimp
  • 2 lemons
  • 8 ounces linguine (half of 1-lb box)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 2 garlic cloves, pressed or minced
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper
  • 16 asparagus spears, woody ends cut off then cut into bite-sized pieces (about 1.5-inches long)
  • 2 tablespoons jarred pesto (or 1/4 cup heavy cream)
  • 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese
  • Serve with: Green salads or fruit

Directions

  1. Pat shrimp dry with paper towel. (Defrost shrimp if frozen first.)
  2. Zest and juice 1 lemon (about 1/4 cup juice). Cut other lemon into wedges.
  3. Place a medium saucepan of well-salted water on the stove to boil and cook linguine according to package directions. Drain and reserve 1 cup of the pasta water.
  4. While water is coming to a boil, heat oil and butter in a large skillet over medium heat, then add garlic and stir, about 30 seconds.
  5. Add shrimp and cook until pink. Flip and add asparagus pieces, cooking another two minutes.
  6. Turn heat to low and add Italian seasoning, salt, pepper, lemon juice, zest, and pesto (or heavy cream).
  7. Add cooked linguine to the skillet and stir everything to combine. Stir in Parmesan cheese and parsley, and drizzle in reserved pasta water a little at a time until you get a creamy texture. Serve immediately with lemon wedges.
Picky Eater TipReserve some pasta before tossing in the sauce, and serve shrimp separately (you can cut the shrimp into smaller pieces if whole shrimp is intimidating).
