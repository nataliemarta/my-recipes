Tofu (or Chicken) Bowls

Serves: 4  ·  Total Time: 1 hour

Ingredients

  • 2 cups uncooked brown or white rice
  • 1 block extra-firm tofu
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 cup each raw, chopped vegetables such as sweet potato, carrots, broccoli, Brussels sprouts, bell peppers, and mushrooms (3 cups total)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/4 cup peanut butter
  • 3 tablespoons real maple syrup
  • 3 tablespoons reduced-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced or pressed
  • 1 tablespoon water, optional
  • crushed peanuts and sliced green onions, to top bowls, if desired

Directions

  1. Press tofu for 30 min to remove excess moisture. Place tofu between paper towels (or a clean kitchen cloth) on a plate. Set a heavy object, like a cast iron pan or pot, on top.
  2. Prepare rice according to package directions.
  3. Preheat oven to 400 degrees F and line two baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone baking mats.
  4. Once tofu is pressed, cut into cubes. Toss cubes in cornstarch and arrange in a single layer on one of the baking sheets.
  5. Chop vegetables into uniform pieces, toss with olive oil, and arrange in a single layer on the second baking sheet.
  6. Bake tofu and vegetables for 20-30 minutes or until tofu is lightly browned and crisp around the edges and vegetables are crisp-tender (or desired doneness). Rotate them between racks halfway through cooking time or bake them separately. Root vegetables such as carrots and potatoes will take longer to cook, followed by broccoli and Brussels sprouts. Add quick-cooking vegetables such as mushrooms and peppers in the last 10 minutes.
  7. While tofu and vegetables are baking, whisk maple syrup through water into the sauce.
  8. Place a portion of rice in each bowl. Top with tofu and vegetables, drizzle Peanut Sauce on top. Top with crushed peanuts and green onion if desired.
Picky Eater TipLet kids build their bowl from what's on the pan, and serve the sauce as a dip.
