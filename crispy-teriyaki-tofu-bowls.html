RMN Crispy Teriyaki Tofu Bowls

Serves: 4  ·  Total Time: 35 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 block extra-firm tofu (or 1 pound chicken)
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 4 teaspoons olive oil
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 4 servings brown or white rice
  • Burman's teriyaki sauce to taste

Directions

  1. Drain and press tofu for 1 hour (place it between two paper towels and put something heavy on top such as a cast iron skillet, draining away excess moisture).
  2. Crumble tofu into a medium bowl with your fingers, breaking it into small, bite-sized pieces.
  3. Top tofu pieces with cornstarch, soy sauce, and 2 teaspoons olive oil and toss to coat.
  4. Heat other 2 teaspoons olive oil in medium skillet over medium heat. Add tofu, pressing it down into a single layer. Cook undisturbed for 10 minutes.
  5. While tofu is cooking, prepare broccoli (steam or stir fry) and rice.
  6. Flip tofu and cook another 10 minutes or until very brown and crispy.
  7. Remove from heat.
  8. Into each bowl, add brown rice, broccoli, and 1/4 of the crispy tofu, and drizzle on teriyaki sauce to taste.
  9. Note: For chicken option, cut 1 pound chicken breasts into bite-sized pieces and cook in a skillet, adding sauce when done.
Picky Eater TipTofu is a fun, futuristic-looking food--the way it can be cut into shapes and can taste like almost anything from scrambled egg to pork ribs. The base flavor is very mild, so serve some without the sauce and offer the teriyaki as a dip instead, or try ketchup, BBQ, or ranch. It might become a favorite!
