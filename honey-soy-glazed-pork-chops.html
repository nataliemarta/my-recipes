Honey-Soy Glazed Pork Chops

Serves: 4  ·  Total Time: 20 minutes

Ingredients

  • Salt and pepper
  • 4 boneless pork chops, about 3/4" thick
  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Serve with: Rice or couscous, sauteed green beans

Directions

  1. Sprinkle salt and pepper on each chop.
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add chops and cook each side until brown and cooked through (I usually remove them when they are still just ever-so-slightly pink because they will continue to cook out of the pan--safe internal temperature for pork chops is 145 degrees).
  3. While the chops are cooking, combine honey, soy sauce, balsamic vinegar, and cornstarch in a bowl and stir until combined.
  4. Transfer cooked chops to a plate.
  5. With heat still on medium, add minced garlic to the skillet and cook, stirring constantly, for about 30 seconds.
  6. Add soy sauce mixture to the pan and let it cook, stirring frequently, for about a minute. It will boil and thicken.
  7. Spoon sauce over chops and serve.
Picky Eater TipServe the sauce on the side or an alternative like plain honey, ketchup, BBQ, ranch, or even a bit of strawberry jam.
