Broiled BBQ Salmon

Serves: 4  ·  Total Time: 15 minutes

Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets (or enough to cut into 4 portions)
  • salt and pepper
  • 1/4 cup barbecue sauce (bottled or homemade)

Directions

  1. Line a sheet pan with foil and move top oven rack to highest position (about six inches from broiler). Set broiler to HIGH.
  2. Place salmon fillets, skin side down, on foil-lined baking sheet. Sprinkle tops with salt and pepper.
  3. Broil for 3 minutes. Remove from oven and brush with thin layer of barbecue sauce.
  4. Return salmon to oven and continue broiling roughly 6-10 minutes more, or until salmon is opaque and flakes with fork. Remove from oven. Brush with another thin coat of barbecue sauce and serve.
  5. Note: Exact broiling time will depend on how thick your salmon is and how well done you like it--check fish throughout the process. Grilling method: Heat to 400 degrees. Brush both sides of fillets with olive oil. Grill skin side up for a few minutes, then flip and brush with sauce, grilling an additional 5-8 minutes or until done.
Picky Eater TipWith a fork, flake the fish apart into bite-sized pieces and start with a small amount. (I know salmon can be expensive, and it's stressful to see it go to waste!)
